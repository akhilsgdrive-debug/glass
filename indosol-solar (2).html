Manufacturing Technology

Advanced Manufacturing Excellence

Every pane of Indosol Solar glass passes through a precisely controlled eight-stage manufacturing workflow, combining raw material science with precision automation.

Raw Material Selection

High-purity silica sand, soda ash, limestone, and refining agents are sourced and tested to strict composition standards.

Glass Melting

Batch materials are melted at temperatures exceeding 1,500°C in advanced float glass furnaces for complete homogenisation.

Pattern Formation

Molten glass passes through precision-engineered rollers that imprint optimised solar emboss patterns for maximum light capture.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is solar patterned glass? +
Solar patterned glass is a specialised glass used as the front cover in photovoltaic modules. Its precision-embossed surface texture scatters and captures light more effectively than flat glass, reducing surface reflection and directing more photons toward the solar cells beneath. This improves module power output without increasing module area.
What is ARC solar glass and how does it work? +
ARC (Anti-Reflective Coated) solar glass features a nano-scale coating applied to the glass surface that minimises light reflected away from the module. The double-layer coating creates destructive interference for reflected light waves, allowing more solar energy to pass through. Indosol Solar's ARC glass achieves ≥94% light transmission, directly increasing the watt-peak output of the finished solar module.
What are the advantages of ultra-clear solar glass over standard glass? +
Ultra-clear solar glass uses low-iron raw materials that eliminate the greenish tint found in standard float glass. This allows significantly more light in the 350–1200nm wavelength range — the spectrum most efficiently converted by solar cells — to reach the photovoltaic layer. The result is higher module efficiency, better performance in diffuse light conditions, and superior long-term power stability.
What solar module technologies are supported by Indosol Solar glass? +
Indosol Solar glass is engineered to be compatible with all mainstream and emerging photovoltaic cell technologies, including Mono-PERC, TOPCon (Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact), HJT (Heterojunction Technology), and bifacial module designs. The glass specifications are also designed to be compatible with future cell architectures such as perovskite-silicon tandems as the industry evolves.
How long does Indosol Solar glass last in the field? +
Indosol Solar glass is designed for a service life of more than 30 years in outdoor photovoltaic installations. The glass is validated through accelerated weathering tests including damp heat (85°C, 85% RH for 1,000 hours), UV exposure, thermal cycling, and hail impact simulation — all conducted in accordance with IEC 61215 and IEC 61730 standards.
What is the production capacity of the Indosol Solar facility? +
The Indosol Solar manufacturing facility is designed for a production capacity of 2,400 Tonnes Per Day (TPD) of solar glass. This output is sufficient to support approximately 10 GW of solar module manufacturing capacity per year, making it one of the largest dedicated solar glass manufacturing plants in India and positioning the company as a critical supplier within the domestic and global solar supply chain.
